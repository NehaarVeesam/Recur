Statement:
You are given an integer array height of length n. There are n vertical lines drawn such that the two endpoints of the ith line are (i, 0) and (i, height[i]).

Find two lines that together with the x-axis form a container, such that the container contains the most water.

Return the maximum amount of water a container can store.

Notice that you may not slant the container.

Approach: Two Pointers
Time Complexity: O(n)
Space Complexity: O(1)
1. To find out most water we calculate the maximum area -> length * breadth 
Here length is the distance between indexes 
breadth is the minimum of both containers height (as water must be contained in between)
2. l = 0 , r= n-1 we iterate whenever left is less than or equal to 
3. We calculate the area as (diff between indexes) * (min of height)
4. To find out the maximum water we move forward from whichever end is less

Learning:
1. When a problem involves an array and asks you to optimize something involving two elements, consider placing one pointer at each end.
2. This greedy choice is correct because moving the taller line cannot improve the answer while the shorter line remains the bottleneck.

# Two Pointer Approach
class Solution:
    def maxArea(self, height: List[int]) -> int:
        l = 0
        r = len(height)-1
        ans = 0
        while l <= r:
            ans = max(ans, min(height[l], height[r])*(r-l))
            if height[l] <= height[r]:
                l = l+1
            else:
                r = r-1
        return ans
